# Philosophy

**Move fast and iterate.** That's the whole thing.

We've spent real time on the design system, tokens, and foundations, enough that
building new screens is quick and consistent. But day to day, week to week, we're not
polishing the system. We **design, ideate, prototype, and ship.** Time isn't sunk into
complex design-system optimization. It's spent getting product in front of people.

## Good enough, on purpose

Quality is staged. Each stage has a target, and chasing more than the target is wasted
time at that point in the process:

<Steps>
  <Step title="80%, good enough for Review">
    Get the idea legible and roughly right. It doesn't need to be pixel-perfect to earn
    useful feedback. It needs to communicate. Bring it to the weekly
    [Review](/handbook/workflow).
  </Step>
  <Step title="95%, ready for devs">
    After feedback, clean it up and hand it off. Organized, annotated, with dev notes
    where they help. Close enough that engineering can build confidently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="100%, together with devs">
    The last 5% happens in collaboration. You follow up, you communicate, and as a
    design engineer you get into the codebase and make the visual edits yourself.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The design engineer

Design doesn't stop at handoff. Once a feature is **developed**, you get your hands
dirty: open the codebase and make the necessary **visual edits there**. The fastest path
from 95% to 100% is usually a few commits, not another Figma round-trip.

<Callout type="note" title="Why this works">
  A stable brand and a published [Foundations](/handbook/files) library are what make
  shipping fast safe. The spine doesn't move, so the surface can.
</Callout>
