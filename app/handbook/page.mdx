# Design Handbook

This is how design works at RateGame. It walks through the process: where work
happens, how it moves from exploration to dev handoff, the tools we lean on, and a
couple of hands-on recipes for the fiddly bits.

RateGame is a community for **rating the best games in sports**, across the NBA, NFL,
MLB, NHL, college, and international soccer. The product has been through **several
iterations of redesign**, and it'll go through more. That's the point. But the **core
brand, the colors, and a handful of key elements are here to stay**, and that stable
spine is what lets everything else move fast.

## One project, many files

Every design lives in a **single RateGame Figma project**. Rather than one giant file
(which is exactly what we outgrew, see [Archive](/handbook/files)), the work is split
into purpose-built files: a place to explore, a place to review, a place to hand off,
libraries for the pieces we reuse, and a few support files.

If you're new here, read these in order:

- **[Philosophy](/handbook/philosophy)**: how we work. Move fast, ship, iterate.
- **[Files](/handbook/files)**: the map of every file and what it's for.
- **[Workflow](/handbook/workflow)**: how a feature travels from idea to dev.
- **[Tools](/handbook/tools)**: what we use alongside Figma.
- **Recipes**: the [achievements](/handbook/recipes/achievements) and
  [flags](/handbook/recipes/flags) workflows, step by step.

<Callout type="tip" title="The short version">
  Explore in **Workboard**, critique in **Review**, then hand the clean version to dev
  in **App** or **Web**. Build on top of the **Foundations** library. Don't
  over-engineer the system. Ship and polish.
</Callout>
